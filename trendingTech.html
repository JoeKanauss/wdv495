Progressive Web Apps

  • Website that acts and feels like a mobile app
  • Better “feel” on mobile devices
  • Takes advantage of website support (plugins, ease of deployment/maintenance) that apps lack
  • More easily discoverable (search engines) than apps (app stores)
  • Examples: Twitter, Washington Post
  • Development of PWAs in 2005, but limitations made them less successful than native applications
  • Mid-2010s, with enhancements with responsive web tools (HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript) became much more successful
